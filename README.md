## Featured Work

### ML-Driven Growth Forecasting Framework - PayPal Credit & Venmo Credit Card
*Data Scientist | 2024 - 2025*

Designed and built PayPal's **first production-grade ML forecasting system** for First-Time Users (FTUs) and Monthly Active Users (MAUs) across four products - PayPal Credit US, PayPal Credit UK, the US Cashback Mastercard, and the Venmo Credit Card - spanning two international markets.

Prior to this initiative, **no structured forecasting methodology existed** for the FTU metric across these products. The MAU approach was Excel-based and manually maintained, producing projections misaligned with actual business outcomes.

**What I built:**
- An end-to-end FTU forecasting system from scratch - including formal metric definition, data framework design, and model deployment - covering New Activations and Reactivations within a single integrated architecture
- A fully automated MAU forecasting pipeline replacing the existing manual process, delivering an **18% improvement in forecast accuracy**
- Models incorporating **deep learning (LSTM), gradient-boosted ensembles, probabilistic Bayesian frameworks, and seasonal decomposition** - each calibrated to the distinct behavioral dynamics of its product and region
- A production **MLOps pipeline** for continuous training, backtesting, deployment, and performance monitoring - reducing manual effort by 45% and deployment time by 55%

**Business impact:**
- FTU and MAU forecasts adopted as the **primary planning baselines** for annual and quarterly cycles across Finance, Marketing, and Risk
- Portfolio grew from **2.95M FTUs / $4.60B TPV (2024)** to **3.26M FTUs / $5.10B TPV (2025)**; 2026 target: 3.35M FTUs / $5.23B TPV
- MAU portfolio: **12.9M users / $31.39B TPV (2024)** to **13.1M users / $31.42B TPV (2025)**; 2026 target: 13.5M / $32.3B
- Methodology recognized internally and extended in 2025 to incorporate Checkout vs. Non-Checkout segmentation
